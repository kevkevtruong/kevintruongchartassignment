A survey by GLAAD shows millennials have a growing discomfort with LGBTQ people

by Kevin Truong

~

A survey released earlier this year shows a growing number of millennials are uncomfortable in personal situations with lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender and queer (LGBTQ) people.

The survey released by the media watchdog group GLAAD included responses from 1,970 adults in the United States ages 18 and older -- including 1,754 individuals who identified as non-LGBTQ.

Respondents were asked how comfortable they were in personal situations with LGBTQ people -- such as learning that a family member is LGBTQ. Among respondents ages 18 to 34, the survey showed an increase in discomfort when compared to the previous two years.

Mariano Ruiz serves on the board of the International Day Against Homophobia, Transphobia and Biphobia. He said the trend could be a product of more divisive discourse from political leaders.

"The rhetoric from politicians always hits at some point the population," said Ruiz. "Not only older people but younger as well."
